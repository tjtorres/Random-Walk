About Random Walk

Explore scenery you enjoy through the photographs of others.

Random Walk allows you to choose or search for a type of scenery and explore areas with photographs that have been identified, through machine learning and image processing, to have similar scenery.

Use the search bar to discover the available scenery classes and bring up markers identified to have the same type of environment on the map.

Or explore and click on the recent Flickr photos to bring up markers to automatically do a map search for that type of scenery.

Use this app to:

  • Find areas around you to explore in your free time.
  • Find interesting locations to visit while traveling.
  • Plan a date or vacation.
  • Discover new areas to take photos.

How does it work?

This app runs by analyzing images in the Yahoo Flickr Creative Commons 100 Million photo dataset as well as the most recent photos uploaded by Flickr users. Once the photos are obtained, they are first filtered so that only those with GPS metadata are kept, and then sent off to be classified in terms of scenery.

The scenery in each image is classified via a cutting edge deep learning technique utilizing a Convolutional Neural Network that has been trained on 2.5 million images (PlacesCNN). The neural network analyzes the pixels in the image and their surrounding pixels to determine important features that are most descriptive of the image's scenery then assigns probabilities to each of 205 scenery classes.

To read more about the inner workings, please see my blog, or view the code for yourself on GitHub.
